## Areas for Improvement

### Acronym Issues
⚠️ **Few terms introduced without full spelling:**
- "HIIT" used without spelling out "High-Intensity Interval Training" until later context clues
- "SIT" introduced as "sprint interval training, SIT" - good but could be clearer
- "RST" (repeated sprint training) mentioned but spelling came after abbreviation
- "RPE" (rate of perceived exertion) used extensively but never fully defined

**Impact:** Moderate - most are inferable from context, but violates the strict spell-first rule

### Technical Term Gaps
⚠️ **Terms that could use better immediate definition:**
- "vVO2max" (16:08) - defined but the "v" for velocity could be more explicit upfront
- "Lactate threshold" (14:50) - used multiple times, practical definition came late
- "Stroke volume" (04:20) - defined as "amount of blood pumped with each beat" but could emphasize WHY it matters more immediately
- "Capitalization" (04:55) - likely transcription error for "capillarization" but shows need for clarity

### Story/Human Element Opportunities
⚠️ **Limited human stories:**
- Stöggl & Sperlich study presented as pure data - no context about the athletes, their experiences, or how they felt
- JAMA cohort study (122,000 patients) presented statistically but no individual narratives
- Helgerud protocol mentioned but no stories of actual participants

**Note:** This may be appropriate given the research material focused on protocols rather than dramatic events (unlike the stablecoin episode). However, even training studies often have interesting human elements:
- What did the threshold group feel vs polarized group?
- Quotes from researchers about surprising findings?
- Stories of dramatic improvements in individual subjects?

### Surprising Moments - Missed Opportunities
⚠️ **Could emphasize more dramatically:**

- The gray zone waste - could quantify: "Spending 50% of your training time in zone 3 means you're wasting 5 hours a week"

### Pacing Concerns
⚠️ **Dense sections:**
- 7-day training blueprint (19:40-23:00) - extremely dense with 7 different protocols
- Supplement section (31:00-32:50) - four supplements with mechanisms and dosing back-to-back
- Could benefit from more "breathing room" or summary statements between dense sections

## Prompt Improvements for Next Time

### For NotebookLM Prompt:

**ADD explicit instruction:**
```
CRITICAL ACRONYM RULE - NEVER use an acronym before spelling it out:
❌ BAD: "RPE should be 8 out of 10"
✅ GOOD: "Your RPE - rate of perceived exertion on a 1-10 scale - should be 8"

Create a mental checklist:
- HRV = Heart Rate Variability
- RPE = Rate of Perceived Exertion
- HIIT = High-Intensity Interval Training
- SIT = Sprint Interval Training
- RST = Repeated Sprint Training

Before using ANY acronym, ask: "Have I spelled this out already?" If no, spell it out.
```

**EMPHASIZE human elements in study presentation:**
```
When presenting research studies:
- Not just: "The study showed X% improvement"
- Instead: "When researchers put 48 athletes through 9 weeks of different protocols, the polarized group reported feeling EASIER during their training yet saw the biggest gains. The threshold group, constantly grinding in zone 3, felt like they were working harder but barely improved. This counter-intuitive finding shocked the research team."

Look for these story elements in studies:
- What surprised the researchers?
- How did participants describe their experience?
- Were there outliers or dramatic responders?
- What made the winning protocol feel different?
```

**ADD more dramatic emphasis:**
```
When presenting shocking findings, PAUSE and emphasize:
- "Here's the stunning part..."
- "Let that sink in for a moment..."
- "To put that in perspective..."

Example:
❌ "A two-week vacation can erase an eight-week training block"
✅ "Here's the devastating truth about detraining: Take a two-week vacation - just 14 days - and you can completely erase EIGHT WEEKS of hard training. That's a 4:1 loss ratio. Every day you're not training, you're losing four days of adaptation."
```

### For Research Prompt:

**ADD request for:**
```
- Researcher quotes or surprising findings mentioned in study discussions
- Individual subject responses or case studies mentioned in papers
- Historical context or discovery stories behind key protocols
- Failed approaches or negative results that led to current understanding
```
